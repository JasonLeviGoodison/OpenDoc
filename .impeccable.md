## Design Context

### Users
Founders sharing pitch decks with investors. Deal teams sharing due diligence documents. Sales teams sharing proposals with prospects. Used during business hours in offices, and late nights preparing materials. Professional context where trust and precision matter more than delight.

### Brand Personality
**Precise, trusted, commanding.** Like a bank vault's nameplate — engraved metal, clean sans, authority without decoration. The interface should feel like a tool built by people who understand what's at stake when you share a confidential document.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark — appropriate for professional tool, long sessions, content-focused work
- **Accent**: Warm amber/gold (OKLCH hue ~70) — evokes trust, premium quality, security. Deliberately NOT the AI-default purple/indigo/cyan.
- **Neutrals**: Tinted warm toward the brand hue for subconscious cohesion
- **Typography**: Manrope — geometric, professional, excellent for data-dense UI. Single font family, hierarchy through weight and size.
- **Anti-references**: Anything with gradient text, purple neon on dark, glassmorphism, identical card grids with rounded icons above headings, centered-everything layouts.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries the hierarchy.** Use weight, size, and spacing — not icons-in-colored-circles — to create visual importance.
2. **Restraint over decoration.** No gradient text, no glow shadows, no radial gradient backgrounds. If it looks like "an AI made this," remove it.
3. **Tinted, not gray.** Every neutral surface is subtly tinted toward the brand amber. Pure gray feels lifeless.
4. **Asymmetry is intentional.** Left-align by default. Break symmetry for emphasis, not as an accident.
5. **Accent is rare.** The amber accent works BECAUSE it's used sparingly — active nav items, CTAs, and sparse inline highlights only.
